# Downloaded Papers

1. [ProbAct: A Probabilistic Activation Function for Deep Neural Networks](1905.10761_ProbAct_Probabilistic_Activation_Function.pdf)
   - Authors: Kumar Shridhar, Joonho Lee, Hideaki Hayashi, et al.
   - Year: 2019
   - arXiv: 1905.10761
   - Why relevant: Introduces stochastic activation by sampling from a Gaussian distribution centered at the activation mean.

2. [Flex-Act: Why Learn when you can Pick?](2601.06441_FlexAct_Why_Learn_When_You_Can_Pick.pdf)
   - Authors: Ramnath Kumar, Kyle Ritscher, Junmin Judy, Lawrence Liu, Cho-Jui Hsieh.
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2601.06441
   - Why relevant: Uses Gumbel-Softmax to dynamically select (sample) the best activation function from a basis set.

3. [Noisy Activation Functions](1603.00391_Noisy_Activation_Functions.pdf)
   - Authors: Caglar Gulcehre, Marcin Moczulski, Misha Denil, Yoshua Bengio.
   - Year: 2016
   - arXiv: 1603.00391
   - Why relevant: Injects noise into the saturated regime of activation functions to improve gradient flow.

4. [Randomized Automatic Differentiation](2007.10412_Randomized_Automatic_Differentiation.pdf)
   - Authors: Deniz Oktay, Nick McGreivy, Joshua Aduol, Alex Beatson, Ryan P. Adams.
   - Year: 2020
   - arXiv: 2007.10412
   - Why relevant: Proposes unbiased gradient estimates through randomized path sampling in the computational graph.

5. [Weight Uncertainty in Neural Networks](1505.05424_Weight_Uncertainty_Neural_Networks.pdf)
   - Authors: Charles Blundell, Julien Cornebise, Koray Kavukcuoglu, Daan Wierstra.
   - Year: 2015
   - arXiv: 1505.05424
   - Why relevant: "Bayes by Backprop" - treats weights as distributions and samples them, providing a framework for stochasticity in NNs.

6. [Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes](1312.6114_VAE_Original_Paper.pdf)
   - Authors: Diederik P Kingma, Max Welling.
   - Year: 2013
   - arXiv: 1312.6114
   - Why relevant: Foundation of stochastic latent representations (reparameterization trick).
